Post-hoc Compression for Gaussian Splatting

Inspired by gradient-informed pruning, our post-hoc method alternates gradient accumulation and pruning + fine-tuning on a pretrained 3DGS model. Each step removes Gaussians with the smallest gradient magnitude; we then recover quality with quantization-aware fine-tuning (SH → uint8, others → FP16) and finally pack parameters with LZMA.

≈30k total iterations
5% pruning per step
1 pass gradient accumulation

Pipeline at a glance

  1. Accumulate gradients on the training set.
  2. Prune Gaussians with the smallest gradients.
  3. Fine-tune with quantization (SH→uint8, others→FP16).
  4. Repeat until the stopping criterion is met; final recovery stage and LZMA compression.
Goal: reduce memory while preserving quality (PSNR/SSIM) and high FPS.
